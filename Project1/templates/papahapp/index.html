Cyber Attacks

Hacking

Unauthorized use of a ones computer that can be used to steal information

An effort to attack a computer system or a private network inside a computer is known as hacking. Simply, it is unauthorized access to or control of computer network security systems with the intention of committing a crime. Hacking is the process of finding some security holes in a computer system or network in order to gain access to personal or corporate information. One example of computer hacking is the use of a password cracking technique to gain access to a computer system. The process of gaining illegal access to a computer system, or a group of computer systems, is known as hacking. This is accomplished by cracking the passwords and codes that grant access to systems. Cracking is the term used to describe the process of obtaining a password or code.

Phishing

Creating fake sites or messages to lure users into giving away information

Phishing is one of the types of cyber attack. Phishing got its name from “phish” meaning fish. It’s a common phenomenon to put bait for the fish to get trapped. Similarly phishing works. It is an unethical way to dupe the user or victim to click on harmful sites. The attacker crafts the harmful site in such a way that the victim feels it to be an authentic site, thus falling prey to it. Once the victim has logged into these fake sites the attacker has access to the login credentials they used and can use those in harmful ways



How Does Phishing occur

  • Clicking on unknown file or attachment Attacker sends a file with maleware injected into the file or attachment that could steal information or harm you device
  • Using open or free wifi hotspot Using public Wifi makes you vulnerable to phishing becasue attackers can intercept your data that you send over a public or unencypted network. For example if you were to log into your bank over a public wifi network attackers could be able to see and steal your login information
  • Responding to social media requests Attackers will often portray like they are someone they are not to try to get you to click on a link they send you on social media. These links could be harmful to your system
  • Clicking on unauthenticated links or ads Harmful malware can be embedded in unauthenticated links and ads and can contain viruses that can steal your information and/or harm you system
